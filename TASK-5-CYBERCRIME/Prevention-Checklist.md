# Stay Safe Online — Cyberbullying Prevention Checklist

    Related Case Study: The Silent Struggle — A Case of Cyberbullying in a College Group Chat



# For Every Student — General Safety

  **Think before you share** — Never post, forward, or comment anything online that you would not say to someone's face in front of a teacher
  
  **Never screenshot or record** anyone during online classes or video calls without their knowledge and consent — this is unethical and illegal under the IT Act, 2000
  
  **Do not forward hurtful content** — If someone shares a mocking image or message in a group, do not forward it. Forwarding makes you equally responsible
  
  **Review your privacy settings** on WhatsApp, Instagram, and other platforms — set your profile to private and control who can add you to groups
  
  **Block and report** anyone who sends you harassing messages — every platform (WhatsApp, Instagram, Snapchat) has a built-in block and report feature



# If You Are Being Cyberbullied

  **Do not respond or retaliate** — Responding often escalates the situation further
  
  **Take screenshots as evidence** — Before blocking or deleting, capture proof of the harassment (date, time, username visible)
  
  **Tell a trusted adult immediately** — Inform a parent, faculty member, or college counsellor as soon as possible
  
  **Report to your college** — Approach the Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) or Student Grievance Cell at your institution
  
  **File an official complaint** at the National Cyber Crime Portal: [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
  
  **Call the helpline** — National Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (available 24x7, free of charge)



# If You Witness Cyberbullying (Bystander Responsibility)

  **Do not laugh, like, or share** the content — even a laughing emoji counts as participation
  
  **Privately support the victim** — Send them a message letting them know you are on their side
  
  **Report the content** on the platform directly using the "Report" button
  
  **Inform a faculty member or college authority** — You do not need the victim's permission to report bullying to a teacher



# Healthy Digital Habits to Prevent Cyberbullying

  **Respect others online** the same way you would in person — treat every message as a permanent record
  
  **Do not join or stay in group chats** where people are being mocked or harassed — leave and report the group
  
  **Educate your peers** — Share awareness about cyberbullying in your class or hostel community
  
  **Check in on quiet classmates** — Sometimes victims suffer in silence; a small act of kindness can make a big difference



# Key Laws You Should Know

 IT Act, 2000 — Section 66A [ Sending offensive or menacing messages electronically ]
 
 IT Act, 2000 — Section 67 [Publishing obscene or humiliating content online ]
 
 IPC Section 509 [ Words or gestures intended to insult the modesty of a person ]
 
 IPC Section 354D [ Cyberstalking — repeatedly following or contacting someone online ]
