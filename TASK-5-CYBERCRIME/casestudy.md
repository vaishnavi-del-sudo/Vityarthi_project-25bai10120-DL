# Case Study: Cyberbullying

**Title: The Silent Struggle — A Case of Cyberbullying in a College Group Chat**



# What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is the use of digital platforms such as social media, messaging apps, or online forums to repeatedly harass, threaten, humiliate, or intimidate another person. Unlike physical bullying, cyberbullying follows the victim everywhere — even into their own home — making it particularly damaging to mental health.



# The Scenario

Priya, an 18-year-old first-year B.Tech student at a college in Indore, was a shy but hardworking student. During her first semester, she answered a question incorrectly during an online class on Google Meet. A classmate, Rohan, took a screenshot of her confused expression and shared it in the class WhatsApp group with a mocking caption. Within minutes, other students began adding jokes and laughing emojis. The post spread to other groups, and within 24 hours, Priya became the target of widespread ridicule online.



# How It Happened — Step by Step

1. **Trigger:** Priya gave a wrong answer during an online class
2. **Screenshot taken:** Rohan captured her video feed without her knowledge or consent
3. **Public humiliation:** The screenshot was shared in a 60-member WhatsApp group with a mocking caption
4. **Escalation:** Other students forwarded it to seniors' groups and added hurtful comments
5. **Continued harassment:** Memes were created using her photo and circulated on Instagram
6. **Isolation:** Priya began being excluded from study groups and ignored in class



# Who is Usually Targeted?

Cyberbullying in college settings most commonly targets:

- Students who are introverted or new to the environment
- Students from smaller towns adjusting to a new city
- Those who make mistakes publicly in online classes
- Students who dress, speak, or behave differently from the majority



# Consequences

Priya stopped attending online classes out of fear and embarrassment. Her academic performance dropped significantly. She developed anxiety and refused to interact with classmates. Her parents noticed she had become withdrawn and took her to a counsellor. The college's Internal Complaints Committee was eventually informed, and Rohan received a formal warning. However, the psychological damage to Priya took months to heal. The screenshots continued to exist online long after the incident was "resolved."



# What Could Have Been Done Differently?

- Rohan should never have taken or shared the screenshot — this is both unethical and potentially illegal under the IT Act, 2000
- Classmates who forwarded it were equally responsible and should have refused to participate
- Priya could have reported it immediately to a trusted faculty member or the college's grievance cell
- The incident should have been reported at [cybercrime.gov.in] or by calling helpline [1930]
